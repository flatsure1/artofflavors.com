Miso Duck Confit

Duck legs slow-cooked in miso-seasoned fat until impossibly tender, then crisped to perfection. French technique meets Japanese soul.

Cure24 hrs
Cook3 hrs
Serves2
LevelAdvanced
🦆

Confit is one of the oldest preservation techniques in French cuisine — submerging meat in its own rendered fat and cooking it low and slow until the collagen breaks down into silk. Here, that tradition meets the umami depth of white miso in a cure that penetrates the duck legs over 24 hours before they ever meet heat.

The result is duck that falls from the bone, carries a faint sweetness from the miso, and shatters into a crackling crisp when you blast the skin in a hot pan. Serve over soft polenta or alongside pickled daikon for a study in contrasts.

The Method

01

The Miso Cure

Mix white miso, sea salt, sugar, garlic, and ginger into a smooth paste. Rub generously over the duck legs, coating every surface. Place in a container, cover, and refrigerate for 24 hours. The miso will draw moisture and begin to season deep into the flesh.

02

Render the Fat

Remove the duck legs and gently wipe off excess miso — a thin coating is fine. In a deep oven-proof dish, melt duck fat gently over low heat until liquid. Add thyme, bay leaves, and crushed garlic. The aromatics will infuse the fat.

03

Slow Confit

Submerge duck legs completely in the warm fat. Cover the dish tightly and transfer to a 90°C oven. Cook for 2.5–3 hours until a skewer passes through with zero resistance. The collagen has fully converted to gelatin at this point.

04

Rest & Store

Allow the duck to cool completely in the fat. For best results, refrigerate overnight in the fat — the texture improves dramatically. The duck will keep in the fat for up to 2 weeks.

05

Crisp the Skin

When ready to serve, remove duck legs from fat and pat skin completely dry. Place skin-side down in a cold, dry pan. Bring to high heat and press down firmly. The skin will render and crisp in 4–5 minutes. Flip briefly to warm through, then rest for 2 minutes before plating.

Ingredients

  • 2Duck legs
  • 4 tbspWhite miso paste
  • 1 tbspSea salt
  • 1 tbspBrown sugar
  • 4 clovesGarlic, minced
  • 20gFresh ginger, grated
  • 500gDuck fat
  • 3 sprigsFresh thyme
  • 2Bay leaves
